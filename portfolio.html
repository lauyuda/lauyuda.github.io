Portfolio Asset Allocation

Examining the correlation table:

Equities versus Bonds

The generated portfolio allocation by Monte Carlo overweights the three bonds (AGG, HYG, and EMB) because of the low/negative correlation with the other equities. This is what would be described as avoiding putting all the eggs into one basket. On the other hand, the actual portfolio allocation took more risk to get a higher return.

USA, Europe, and Emerging Markets(EM)

These three equities are highly correlated with one another and has high correlation to other equities as well. It is likely that USA has a superior return-to-risk ratio to the other two which explains for Europe and EM to be underweighted in all Monte Carlo simulated portfolio. In the actual portfolio, EM had a much higher weight.

China

China likely has high variance but relatively higher returns which explains that China had less than 1% allocation in the minimum variance portfolio but 10.9% and 23.3% in the 5 years max Sharpe and 10 years max Sharpe portfolio respectively.

Japan and Frontier Markets(FM)

Both Japan and FM have correlations which can be described to fall in the mid-range. In terms of diversification, it makes them relevant in reducing variance and hold about 10% each in the simulated minimum variance portfolio. However, lack of confidence in the FM and the underperforming Japanese market meant that they have very low weights in the actual portfolio.

Singapore

Singapore likely has low returns and high standard deviation which causes it to be unfavourable in both minimum variance and maximum Sharpe ratio portfolios. It was kept at the minimum of 5% in the actual portfolio due to a constraint by the virtual client.

Minimum Variance Portfolio

It is interesting to note that the 5 years and 10 years minimum variance portfolio remained the same whereas there was quite a change for a max Sharpe ratio based portfolio. This suggests that it may be more "stable and consistent" to use the minimum variance portfolio.

Technical Analysis

Bollinger Band for CMC200:

The price of CMC200 double in less than one month and I looked into should the portfolio include crypto out of FOMO. Bollinger bands consist of a centerline and two price channels (bands) above and below it. The centerline is a moving average; the price channels are the standard deviations (usually 2 std dev) of the ticker. The bands will widen as the stock becomes more volatile.

When CMC200 prices continually touch the upper Bollinger band, the prices are thought to be overbought; conversely, when they continually touch the lower band, prices are thought to be oversold, triggering a buy signal. As evident, it is not a good idea to buy CMC200 due to rising prices and FOMO.

After this analysis, the price continued climbing despite showing signs of overbuying. One can never win bets 100% of the time.
